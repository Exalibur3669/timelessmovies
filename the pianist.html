    • The Pianist

    Not a celebration of the human spirit, neither demonizing the Germans nor ionizing the Jews, resisting both deceptive moral uplift and despairing moral nihilism, The Pianist is a work of exquisite restraint.

    Critic Reception

  • Geoff Andrew: Old-fashioned in both visual and narrative style and in its overall restraint, the film clearly benefits from the director's first-hand knowledge of the territory.
  • Fico Cangiano: An obligatory watch for any true cinephiles and film history buffs.
